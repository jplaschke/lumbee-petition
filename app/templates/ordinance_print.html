ENACTING CLAUSE

WE, THE UNDERSIGNED ENROLLED MEMBERS of the Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina, exercising our rights under Article V of the Constitution of the Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina, do hereby propose and submit this Ordinance for consideration by the General Membership of the Tribe. This Ordinance is submitted as a citizen initiative and shall not take effect unless and until it is approved by the General Membership through the initiative process established by the Lumbee Constitution.

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina is a federally recognized Indian tribe with inherent sovereign authority over its members and territory;
WHEREAS, Article V of the Constitution of the Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina grants enrolled members the right to propose ordinances through the initiative process;
WHEREAS, gaming operations, when properly governed, represent a significant economic development opportunity for the Tribe and its enrolled members;
WHEREAS, the enrolled members of the Lumbee Tribe desire a gaming governance framework that ensures transparency, accountability, and direct benefit to all enrolled members;
WHEREAS, this Ordinance is designed to protect the sovereign interests of the Tribe while maximizing economic benefits for enrolled members and the broader Lumbee community;
NOW, THEREFORE, the undersigned enrolled members hereby propose the following Ordinance for approval by the General Membership of the Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina.

PROPOSED ORDINANCE TEXT

ARTICLE I — GENERAL PROVISIONS

PROPOSED SECTION 1.
Short Title
This Ordinance shall be known and cited as the "Lumbee Gaming Governance, Transparency & Economic Development Act."
PROPOSED SECTION 2.
Purpose
The purpose of this Ordinance is to establish a comprehensive framework for the governance, regulation, and operation of gaming activities by the Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina in a manner that:
  1. Protects the sovereign interests of the Tribe;
  2. Ensures transparency and accountability in all gaming operations;
  3. Maximizes economic benefits for enrolled members;
  4. Provides meaningful oversight by enrolled members;
  5. Complies with applicable federal law, including the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act.
PROPOSED SECTION 3.
Definitions
As used in this Ordinance:
  • "Tribe" means the Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina.
  • "Tribal Council" means the governing body of the Lumbee Tribe.
  • "Commission" means the Lumbee Gaming Commission established herein.
  • "Gaming Operation" means any gaming activity conducted under Tribal authority.
  • "Enrolled Member" means a person duly enrolled on the official roll of the Lumbee Tribe.
  • "Net Gaming Revenue" means gross gaming revenue less operating expenses, debt service, and required reserves.
  • "IGRA" means the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act, 25 U.S.C. § 2701 et seq.
  • "NIGC" means the National Indian Gaming Commission.
PROPOSED SECTION 4.
Sovereign Authority
This Ordinance is enacted pursuant to the inherent sovereign authority of the Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina and the authority granted to enrolled members under Article V of the Lumbee Constitution. Nothing in this Ordinance shall be construed to diminish or waive the sovereign immunity of the Tribe.
PROPOSED SECTION 5.
Scope of Application
This Ordinance applies to all gaming activities conducted by or on behalf of the Lumbee Tribe, all gaming facilities located on Tribal lands, all gaming management contractors, and all persons employed in or associated with Tribal gaming operations.

ARTICLE II — LUMBEE GAMING COMMISSION

PROPOSED SECTION 6.
Establishment
There is hereby established the Lumbee Gaming Commission, an independent regulatory body of the Tribe, responsible for the oversight, regulation, and licensing of all Tribal gaming operations.
PROPOSED SECTION 7.
Composition
The Commission shall consist of five (5) Commissioners:
  1. Three (3) Commissioners elected by the General Membership;
  2. One (1) Commissioner appointed by the Tribal Council;
  3. One (1) independent Commissioner with gaming regulatory expertise, approved by the General Membership.
All elected Commissioners must be enrolled members of the Lumbee Tribe. No Commissioner may hold any other Tribal government position.
PROPOSED SECTION 8.
Terms and Removal
Commissioners shall serve staggered four-year terms. Commissioners may be removed by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the General Membership for cause, including malfeasance, neglect of duty, or breach of fiduciary obligation.
PROPOSED SECTION 9.
Powers and Duties
The Commission shall have authority to:
  1. Issue, suspend, and revoke gaming licenses;
  2. Promulgate gaming regulations;
  3. Conduct investigations and audits;
  4. Impose civil fines and sanctions;
  5. Approve all gaming management contracts;
  6. Report quarterly to the General Membership;
  7. Enforce all provisions of this Ordinance and applicable federal law.
PROPOSED SECTION 10.
Independence
The Commission shall operate independently of the Tribal Council and Tribal gaming management. No Tribal Council member, gaming enterprise employee, or gaming contractor may direct or interfere with Commission regulatory decisions.

ARTICLE III — ETHICS & TRANSPARENCY

PROPOSED SECTION 11.
Conflict of Interest
No Commissioner, Tribal Council member, or gaming enterprise officer shall have a direct or indirect financial interest in any gaming contractor, vendor, or supplier doing business with the Tribe's gaming operations. Violations shall result in immediate removal and referral for prosecution.
PROPOSED SECTION 12.
Financial Disclosure
All Commissioners, gaming enterprise officers, and Tribal Council members involved in gaming decisions shall file annual financial disclosure statements. These statements shall be available for inspection by any enrolled member upon request.
PROPOSED SECTION 13.
Open Records
All Commission meeting minutes, licensing decisions, audit reports, and financial statements of the gaming enterprise shall be public records accessible to all enrolled members. Records shall be posted on the Tribal website within thirty (30) days of completion.
PROPOSED SECTION 14.
Whistleblower Protection
Any enrolled member or gaming enterprise employee who reports in good faith a violation of this Ordinance or applicable gaming law shall be protected from retaliation. Retaliation against a whistleblower shall constitute a separate violation subject to civil and criminal penalties.
PROPOSED SECTION 15.
Anti-Corruption Provisions
Any person who offers, gives, solicits, or receives any bribe, kickback, or improper payment in connection with Tribal gaming operations shall be subject to immediate termination, civil liability, and referral to federal law enforcement.

ARTICLE IV — LAND & DEVELOPMENT

PROPOSED SECTION 16.
Gaming Lands
Gaming operations shall be conducted only on lands that constitute "Indian lands" as defined under IGRA, 25 U.S.C. § 2703(4). The Tribal Council shall pursue all available federal processes to establish eligible gaming lands in Robeson County, North Carolina.
PROPOSED SECTION 17.
Land Acquisition
Any land acquisition for gaming purposes shall require:
  1. Approval by the General Membership by majority vote;
  2. Environmental and cultural resource review;
  3. Compliance with all applicable federal land-into-trust requirements;
  4. Public notice to enrolled members not less than sixty (60) days prior to vote.
PROPOSED SECTION 18.
Facility Standards
All gaming facilities shall be designed, constructed, and maintained to standards that reflect the dignity and cultural heritage of the Lumbee people. The Commission shall establish minimum facility standards by regulation within one hundred eighty (180) days of this Ordinance taking effect.
PROPOSED SECTION 19.
Cultural Preservation
Gaming facility development shall include provisions for the display and celebration of Lumbee history, art, and culture. Not less than one percent (1%) of facility construction costs shall be dedicated to cultural preservation elements within the facility.
PROPOSED SECTION 20.
Environmental Protection
All gaming development shall comply with applicable environmental protection standards. The Tribe shall conduct environmental impact assessments prior to any major construction and shall protect the natural resources of the Lumbee homeland.

ARTICLE V — FINANCING & CONTRACTS

PROPOSED SECTION 21.
Financing Authority
The Tribe may finance gaming development through Tribal funds, federal grants, bonds, or commercial financing. Any financing arrangement exceeding five million dollars ($5,000,000) shall require approval by the General Membership.
PROPOSED SECTION 22.
Management Contracts
Any management contract for gaming operations shall:
  1. Be approved by the NIGC pursuant to 25 U.S.C. § 2711;
  2. Be approved by the General Membership;
  3. Not exceed five (5) years in initial term;
  4. Provide for Tribal buyout rights;
  5. Require the contractor to train and transition management to Tribal members.
PROPOSED SECTION 23.
Vendor Licensing
All vendors, suppliers, and contractors providing goods or services to gaming operations shall be licensed by the Commission. Preference shall be given to enrolled members and Tribally-owned businesses in all procurement decisions.
PROPOSED SECTION 24.
Debt Limitations
Total gaming enterprise debt shall not exceed three (3) times annual net gaming revenue without approval by the General Membership. The Commission shall monitor debt levels quarterly and report to the General Membership annually.
PROPOSED SECTION 25.
Tribal Employment Preference
The gaming enterprise shall adopt and enforce a Tribal member employment preference policy. Not less than fifty percent (50%) of all gaming enterprise positions shall be filled by enrolled members within three (3) years of operations commencing.

ARTICLE VI — REVENUE DISTRIBUTION

PROPOSED SECTION 26.
Revenue Allocation Framework
Net Gaming Revenue shall be allocated as follows:
Purpose Allocation
Tribal Government Operations 25%
Tribal Member Per Capita Distributions 25%
Education Fund 20%
Healthcare Fund 15%
Economic Development & Housing 10%
Gaming Enterprise Reserve 5%
Allocations may be adjusted by the General Membership by majority vote not more than once per fiscal year.
PROPOSED SECTION 27.
Per Capita Distribution Plan
Per capita distributions to enrolled members shall be governed by a Per Capita Distribution Plan approved by the General Membership and the Secretary of the Interior pursuant to 25 U.S.C. § 2710(b)(3). Distributions shall be made no less than quarterly.
PROPOSED SECTION 28.
Education Fund
The Education Fund shall provide scholarships, vocational training, and educational support to enrolled members and their dependents. Fund governance shall include a board with enrolled member majority.
PROPOSED SECTION 29.
Healthcare Fund
The Healthcare Fund shall supplement Indian Health Service funding to provide comprehensive healthcare services to enrolled members. Priority shall be given to elder care, mental health, and substance abuse treatment.
PROPOSED SECTION 30.
Economic Development Fund
The Economic Development and Housing Fund shall finance affordable housing construction, small business loans for enrolled members, and infrastructure improvements in Lumbee communities throughout Robeson County.

ARTICLE VII — OVERSIGHT, AUDITS & MEMBER RIGHTS

PROPOSED SECTION 31.
Annual Independent Audit
The gaming enterprise shall be subject to an annual independent financial audit conducted by a certified public accounting firm selected by the Commission. Audit reports shall be provided to the NIGC and made available to all enrolled members within ninety (90) days of fiscal year end.
PROPOSED SECTION 32.
Member Inspection Rights
Any enrolled member may request inspection of gaming enterprise financial records, Commission meeting minutes, and licensing records. Requests shall be fulfilled within thirty (30) days. The Commission may redact only information protected by federal law or ongoing investigations.
PROPOSED SECTION 33.
Annual Member Report
The Commission shall publish and distribute an Annual Member Report to all enrolled members detailing gaming revenues, expenditures, distributions, employment statistics, and Commission activities for the prior fiscal year.
PROPOSED SECTION 34.
Member Petition for Audit
Upon petition by not less than five percent (5%) of enrolled members, the Commission shall conduct a special audit of any aspect of gaming operations. The cost of such audit shall be borne by the gaming enterprise.
PROPOSED SECTION 35.
Quarterly Financial Reports
The gaming enterprise shall publish quarterly financial reports within forty-five (45) days of each quarter's end. Reports shall include gross revenue, operating expenses, net revenue, and distributions made during the quarter.
PROPOSED SECTION 36.
Performance Standards
The Commission shall establish annual performance benchmarks for the gaming enterprise. Failure to meet benchmarks for two (2) consecutive years shall trigger a mandatory General Membership review of gaming management.
PROPOSED SECTION 37.
Internal Controls
The Commission shall adopt Minimum Internal Control Standards (MICS) consistent with NIGC requirements. All gaming operations shall implement and maintain internal controls approved by the Commission.
PROPOSED SECTION 38.
Compliance Monitoring
The Commission shall conduct unannounced compliance inspections of gaming facilities not less than quarterly. Inspection reports shall be posted on the Tribal website within fifteen (15) days of completion.

ARTICLE VIII — MEMBER APPROVAL REQUIREMENTS

PROPOSED SECTION 39.
Matters Requiring Member Approval
The following actions shall require approval by the General Membership before taking effect:
  1. Any amendment to this Ordinance;
  2. Any gaming management contract or amendment thereto;
  3. Any land acquisition for gaming purposes;
  4. Any financing arrangement exceeding $5,000,000;
  5. Any change to the revenue allocation framework;
  6. Any compact with the State of North Carolina;
  7. Expansion of gaming to new locations or game types;
  8. Any agreement waiving Tribal sovereign immunity related to gaming.
PROPOSED SECTION 40.
Voting Procedures
Member votes required under this Ordinance shall be conducted by secret ballot. Absentee voting shall be available to all enrolled members. Results shall be certified by an independent election monitor and posted publicly within seventy-two (72) hours of vote completion.
PROPOSED SECTION 41.
Quorum Requirements
Member votes shall be valid when not less than twenty percent (20%) of enrolled members in good standing participate. Approval shall require a majority of votes cast unless a supermajority is specified elsewhere in this Ordinance.
PROPOSED SECTION 42.
Notice Requirements
Not less than sixty (60) days written notice shall be provided to all enrolled members prior to any vote required under this Ordinance. Notice shall include the full text of the matter to be voted upon and a plain-language summary.

ARTICLE IX — LICENSING & BACKGROUND INVESTIGATIONS

PROPOSED SECTION 43.
License Requirements
No person shall be employed in a key or primary management position in Tribal gaming operations without a valid license issued by the Commission. License applications shall include a comprehensive background investigation consistent with NIGC requirements.
PROPOSED SECTION 44.
Suitability Standards
The Commission shall deny a license to any applicant who:
  1. Has been convicted of a felony involving dishonesty or breach of trust;
  2. Has been found to have violated any gaming law or regulation;
  3. Has associations with organized crime;
  4. Has provided false information in the license application.
PROPOSED SECTION 45.
License Renewal
All gaming licenses shall be renewed annually. The Commission shall conduct background re-investigations for key management positions every three (3) years or upon a material change in circumstances.

ARTICLE X — RESPONSIBLE GAMING

PROPOSED SECTION 46.
Responsible Gaming Program
The gaming enterprise shall establish and maintain a comprehensive responsible gaming program including self-exclusion lists, problem gambling awareness, and referral services to treatment programs. Not less than one percent (1%) of net gaming revenue shall fund responsible gaming and treatment programs.
PROPOSED SECTION 47.
Member Protections
Enrolled members shall have the right to place themselves on a voluntary self-exclusion list that prohibits them from gaming at Tribal facilities. The enterprise shall implement technical controls to enforce self-exclusion. Violations by the enterprise shall result in civil liability.
PROPOSED SECTION 48.
Minimum Age
No person under the age of twenty-one (21) years shall be permitted to participate in gaming activities at Tribal gaming facilities. The gaming enterprise shall implement age verification procedures approved by the Commission.

ARTICLE XI — AMENDMENTS, SEVERABILITY & EFFECTIVE DATE

PROPOSED SECTION 49.
Amendments
This Ordinance may be amended only by a majority vote of the General Membership following the notice procedures set forth in Proposed Section 42. No amendment shall take effect until approved by the General Membership and, where required, by the NIGC.
PROPOSED SECTION 50.
Severability
If any provision of this Ordinance or its application to any person or circumstance is held invalid, the remainder of the Ordinance and the application of its provisions to other persons or circumstances shall not be affected.
PROPOSED SECTION 51.
Supremacy of Federal Law
Nothing in this Ordinance shall be construed to conflict with or supersede applicable federal law, including IGRA and NIGC regulations. In the event of any conflict, federal law shall control.
PROPOSED SECTION 52.
Effective Date
This Ordinance shall take effect upon:
  1. Approval by the General Membership of the Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina through the initiative process; and
  2. Where required by IGRA, approval by the Chairman of the National Indian Gaming Commission.
Until both conditions are satisfied, this document remains a proposed ordinance only and shall have no legal force or effect.
